2.2 Plug ejection / plunger drive / juice collection

Plunger, filter, plug cutter interface; plug ejection into disposal core chute.

Plug ejection (extraction cycle)

  1. Pressing stroke: Juice leaves the oranges into the branched Y-section of the collector. The plug remains in the plug cutter / filter area.
  2. Plug ejection: After the pressing stroke, the plug ejection system pushes a plunger through the filter, pushes the plug back out of the plug cutter, and the plug falls into the core chute of the disposal system (see Figure 1, Figure 2, Figure 6).
  3. Disposal: Meanwhile, peels fall to the sides of the peelers, avoid the core chutes, two channels; the two augers take peels and plugs/cores away separately (Outflow-disposal).

Components

  • Plunger — pushes through filter to eject plug (multiple in parallel)
  • Plunger actuator bar / plate — light purple; carries plungers in unison
  • Plug ejection channels/housings — green; filter/plug-cutter interface → core chute
  • Actuator rods — red, yellow — connect to drive
  • Guides/bushings — orange, light blue
  • Return spring — yellow helical (return motion)
  • Filter — plunger passes through; juice to collector
  • Plug cutter — (in Collection); plug ejected by this system
  • Plunger drive bracket

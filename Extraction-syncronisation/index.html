2.1b Extraction & synchronisation

Driven peelers, driven bracket (yoke mounts), fruit support, roller, chute deflectors. Stages oranges from the feeder and meshes with static peelers for extraction. Part of intake (2.1).

Function

  • Fruit support directs fruit from the pipe to the peeler and reduces risk of fruit falling to the sides; oranges fall onto it as they are staged to drop into the peelers’ jaws (see Fruit support detail below).
  • Roller pushes open a spring on the feeder system that drops an orange onto the fruit support.
  • Driven peelers mesh with the static peelers (collection) to process fruit; the driven bracket has to handle a lot of load from the yokes and mashes against the static peelers.
  • Chute deflectors redirect peel from the base of the peeler to the back of the bin; mounted to the driven bracket; bent plate design; they also prevent peel from falling into the chutes during the compression stroke of the juicing cycle.
  • Yokes connect to the 2 sets of 4× mount holes on the back of the driven bracket (see Figure 6); drive comes from transmission / drivetrain.
Peel deflectors (WIP): The chute deflector (this subsystem) and the static deflector (collection) are both work in progress. Ensure they are easily manufacturable, do not collide during motion, and are rigid and clean.

Fruit support (detail)

Purpose: To direct fruit from the pipe to the peeler and remove risk of fruit falling to the sides.

  • 3 pairs of flat angled rails. Height is tallest at the ends and tapers down to lowest at the center so oranges roll towards the center two rails. Length of rails varies to match tube diameter.
  • Connecting link to maintain spacing and parallelism between rails and perpendicularity to the plate.
  • Indexing slots with varying spacing for inserting rails.
  • Top edge of rails must be filleted to prevent cutting of fruit.
  • Base is bolted to the drive bracket for easy removal (ideal for regular replacing of rollers).

Design for CIP: The angled plates are welded together as rails so the clean-in-place system can spray through the gaps to clean the two peelers below.

Rail angle: Rails are angled downwards so oranges roll towards the front. Current angle is 3°; increase to 8° required because a 5° downwards slope was added to the top face of the driven bracket. From the front view, the fruit support rails are higher on the sides than the center rails, ensuring oranges roll towards the center.

Pusher: The pusher is where the roller is attached using a bolt. This part was designed as cut and welded sheet steel but could be made as a formed component.

Interfaces

  • Input (motion): Yokes from transmission connect to 2 sets of 4× mount holes on back of driven bracket.
  • Input (fruit): Feeder drops oranges via roller/spring onto fruit support.
  • Output: Fruit drops into peelers’ jaws; driven peelers mesh with static peelers (collection) for extraction.

Introdução

Stages oranges from the feeder and meshes with static descascadores for extraction. Yokes from transmissão drive the bracket and descascadores.

Peel defletores: The chute deflector (this subsystem) under the driven peeler modules is the deflector required to cover the core chutes during compression and route peel correctly. Static defletores on the collection mount plate are removed (figures/text refresh pending).

Discussão

Design preliminar e intenção

  • What this subsystem does — Receive single oranges from the feeder and stage them on the suporte de frutas, then drive the extraction event by meshing driven descascadores with static descascadores (collection). The transmissão yoke/shafts push this module into the collection module during compression.
  • Design status — Peeler core geometry is inherited from a commercial machine. The mounting flanges for both driven and static descascadores (and how they are manufactured) are not finalized and are planned as machined-on flanges added to existing descascadores.
  • Hygiene/washdown — This is inside the splash zone. All surfaces should be spray-down washable with no pockets/shelves; top faces should be sloped for drainage. Fasteners should follow hygienic practice (e.g., avoid Phillips/slotted where possible; prefer hex heads).

Problemas e riscos conhecidos

  • High force path — The suporte acionado carries large loads and bending moments: transmissão shafts push on a short bracket while supporting four peeler modules. Stiffness and fatigue life are critical.
  • Peeler alignment — Driven descascadores must be concentric/aligned with static descascadores. Current plan relies on an adjust-then-lock approach (loose fit holes for adjustment, then drill/ream and add shoulder bolts/dowels).
  • Synchronization pusher stiffness — Only one pusher is used across four modules, pushing a diagonal spring. If the pusher or suporte de frutas flexes laterally (X-axis), it can collide with tubes/lids and cause catastrophic damage.
  • Defletores are WIP — Chute defletores must prevent peel from falling into core chutes during compression and redirect peel into peel chute; current concept is not yet constrained/stiff.

DFM e fabricação (China)

  • Suporte acionado — Current intent is a sand-cast suporte acionado with draft angles and post-machining for critical mounting faces/holes. Contractor to confirm rib thickness, casting quality, and whether cast vs welded is best.
  • Surface finishing — Consider post-processing for the cast bracket to improve cleanability (e.g., smoothing/flow finishing) so there are no crevices where pulp can accumulate.
  • Peeler flange fabrication — Plan is to machine flanges (lathe) and weld a plate to existing descascadores for bolt-up. Contractor to propose a robust welding + inspection approach that preserves geometry and hygiene.
  • Suporte de frutas — Current design uses waterjet-cut ~5 mm plates assembled with slots and welded. Stiffness must prevent sag and lateral flex, while allowing CIP spray through rails/gaps.

Otimizações buscadas

  • Alignment repeatability — Fast alignment procedure that can be repeated seasonally (adjust → lock → dowel/shoulder-bolt).
  • Cleanability — No pockets; sloped top faces; rail gaps that allow spray-through cleaning of descascadores.
  • Stiffness — Increase stiffness of suporte acionado and suporte de frutas to prevent collisions and preserve peeler alignment.

Perguntas para o contratado

  1. Propose the best manufacturing strategy for the suporte acionado (cast vs welded) given washdown/hygiene requirements, stiffness needs, and China supply chain.
  2. Propose a peeler alignment procedure and tolerances for concentricity/mesh so descascadores do not collide and wear is controlled.
  3. Review the suporte de frutas + pusher stiffness requirements and propose design changes that prevent lateral flex and collisions while keeping CIP spray-through.
  4. Propose a chute deflector design that is stiff, manufacturable (formed/bent), and does not collide during motion; ensure peel is routed to peel chute (not core chute).

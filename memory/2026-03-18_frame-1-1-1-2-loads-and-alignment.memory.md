## Frame 1.1 + 1.2 updates — weldment, loads, alignment (2026-03-18)

### Frame (1.1) — current design + uncertainties
- Frame is a weldment of **80×80×5 GB/T square tube** with **GB/T #8 C-channel/C-brackets** (China-available). Two side rails + C-bracket cross-members; corner gussets concept (80×80) but contractor to propose best weld configuration.
- Status: **preliminary CAD**, current height set, not released.
- Biggest risk: **weld distortion/flatness** at top interfaces; goal is for the **three main mount plates** (drivetrain/transmission/collection) to be **adjustable/shimmable** to compensate.
- Key open interface: **under-machine clearance** for 3rd-party peel/core augers under peel chute; constrained by a lower cross-beam and leg spacing.
- Another open issue: collection/plunger cover/lid concept; current upward-opening lid blocks operator access close to front; may drive rail/geometry changes.
- Environment: frame exposed to vapors/cleaning agents (outside main extraction chamber). Surface treatment should be corrosion resistant but not highest priority vs alignment.

### Loads and load paths (1.2) — critical load sources and concerns
- Prime mover: **WEG W21/W22 IE3** motor (product **13533507**), **7.5 kW (10 hp)**, 50 Hz, 4-pole, **1460 rpm rated**, **~49 N·m rated torque**, 38 mm shaft.
- Drive: SPA belt, **1:1 pulleys** (170 mm pitch), datum dia 164.5 mm, belt speed ~13 m/s, **two belts planned**, center distance TBD (affects belt tension/radial loads).
- Reducer: Sumitomo (“6000” as stated) output ~70 rpm. Preliminary drivetrain calc: **~1023 N·m** at driven gear and estimated **~6.6 kN tangential / ~7.2 kN normal** gear forces (contractor to validate with final gear geometry).
- Concentrated load areas: drivetrain tapered roller bearing + mount plate; cyclic cam-yoke reciprocation; peak compression event.
- Transmission guidance: two shafts through **LMK35UU (35 mm) linear bearings**. Concern about **asymmetric/jam case** causing shaft twist and high moments; bearings sit high above plate → overturning moments into mount plate and frame; dowel/shear pins + bolts must handle shear/moment/vibration.

### Alignment / repeatability requirement
- Extraction (driven peelers) must align concentrically with collection-side peelers. Plan: align plates and peelers, then **dowel/ream** so reassembly preserves alignment.
- Contractor is asked to propose datum scheme, joint strategy (dowels/bolts), weld/jig approach, and acceptance targets for deflection/misalignment.

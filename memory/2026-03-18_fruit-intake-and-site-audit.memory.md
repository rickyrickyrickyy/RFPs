## Fruit intake system fill + site consistency audit (2026-03-18)

### Fruit intake system (Subsystem 6) — new details captured
- **Goal**: drop **one orange at a time** per station reliably into fruit support / peelers.
- **Upstream context**: oranges come from feed lines after a size sorter into **entry tubes**.
- **Entry tubes requirements**: adjustable height, rigid when locked, easy remove/reinstall; designed with **pinned hinge joints** (two rotation points) to accommodate height variation.
- **Feeding sequence**:
  1) orange column enters entry tube and 90° tube,
  2) bottom orange rests on **synchronization spring**; column stacks above,
  3) during compression, pusher opens spring so one fruit drops onto fruit support,
  4) on return, spring closes to hold next fruit while staged fruit drops into peelers.
- **Modularity**: 90° tube + straight tube + spring mounts are riveted to a per-station mount plate; mount plate riveted to feeder body so tubes can be swapped by drilling out rivets.
- **CIP**: 90° tube has a top port/tube intended for a nozzle spraying down into entry tube/spring zone (nozzle specifics TBD).
- **Feeder body/lid**: lid is ~2 mm sheet steel concept with diagonal cutouts for mount plates and bracing to prevent buckling; hinge loads should land on bracing, not thin sheet. Lid lip should overhang inward to keep spray/juice inside extraction zone.

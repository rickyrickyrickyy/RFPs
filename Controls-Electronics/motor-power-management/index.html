Motor power management

Part of the Controls and electronics section. Power distribution, protection, and sensing for the drivetrain motor in a washdown environment.

Introduction

This page defines the electrical power/control items needed to run and protect the drivetrain motor, and to provide current sensing for spike detection and throughput estimation. The design must be suitable for industrial cleaning (water protection) and for a 50 Hz China installation baseline.

Control and protection requirements

  • Local operation — local ON/OFF and run/stop (stand-alone).
  • LOTO — rear lockout/tagout isolation point.
  • Safety interlocks — fail-safe lid switches and stop-with-peelers-open logic.
  • Water protection — enclosures, glands, routing suitable for rigorous washdown.

Sensing

  • Motor current sensor — instantaneous current measurement for spike detection and rough throughput estimate (fruit processed per period).
  • Cycle sensor — cycle count per period (may share hardware with yoke position / open-peeler stop sensor).

Questions for contractor

  1. Propose a washdown-safe electrical architecture (cabinet IP rating, glands, routing) suitable for a China plant.
  2. Specify the motor starter/contactor and whether a soft starter is required; include overcurrent protection sizing based on motor current/duty assumptions.
  3. Specify current sensing hardware and how it will be used for spike detection and throughput estimation.
  4. Confirm inlet connector(s) and wiring approach for 380/415 V 3-phase 50 Hz (CN baseline), and advise how to keep a single design compatible with 60 Hz markets where possible.

Interfaces

  • Input: plant power (3-phase), operator commands, safety interlocks.
  • Output: protected power to drivetrain motor; sensor signals to control logic/IoT export.
  • Mount: internal control cabinet/panel.

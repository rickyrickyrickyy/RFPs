3. General design and manufacturing (RFP scope)

Cross-cutting scope for all subsystems. For food-safety and surface requirements, see Project overview → General design, manufacturing, and food-safety requirements.

Material selection

Every component must have a specified material. Choose materials to suit function, load, environment (including food and splash zones), and cost. Document material per part or subassembly in each subsystem RFP.

Manufacturing method and manufacturability

For each part, decide the manufacturing method (e.g. laser/plasma cut, bent sheet, machining, casting, weldment) and optimize for manufacturability. Consider complexity, volume, and tolerances. Prefer stock sizes and simple operations where possible.

Fastener selection and assembly process

All fasteners must be selected (type, size, grade) and the assembly process for every subassembly and the full machine must be defined. Minimise variety of fastener sizes; design for clear access, tooling, and repeatable torque. See Overview for food-zone rules (no exposed threads in food/splash zones, standoffs, etc.).

Tolerancing

Tolerancing between components is critical for fit, alignment, and bearing life. Specify tolerances that are sufficient for function and agree with manufacturers how they will be achieved (process capability, inspection, rework). Document critical interfaces in each subsystem.

Post-processing

Select post-processing (e.g. grinding, honing, polishing, deburring, passivation) where needed to achieve surface finish, dimensions, or food-safety requirements. Specify per part or zone; manufacturers may propose methods that meet the spec.

CAD: Inventor → SolidWorks

Parts are currently drawn in Inventor. The project is switching to SolidWorks. New and revised deliverables should be in SolidWorks; migration and conversion of existing Inventor data to be planned as needed.

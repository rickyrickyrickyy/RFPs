How subassemblies fit together

Introduction to the assembly flow: how fruit moves through the machine and how each subsystem and subassembly connects.

Product flow (fruit → juice and waste)

  1. Intake feeder — Oranges enter via external duct/hopper. The feeder body (on its hinge) supports the feeding subassembly and entry tubes. Sync springs drop fruit one at a time onto the fruit support.
  2. Extraction & synchronisation — Fruit support stages each orange; driven peelers (on the driven bracket) mesh with static peelers. Chute deflectors (extraction) direct peel to the back of the bin. Fingers interlace; peel goes to disposal; juice and plug stay with the collection side.
  3. Collection — Static peelers, juice collectors (Y-tubes), filter tube, and plug cutter are on the collection (loaded) mount plate. Juice flows through filter slots into collectors; the centre plug is held in the plug cutter. Static deflectors direct peel to the front of the bin.
  4. Plug ejection — After the pressing stroke, the plunger (driven by the plunger drive bracket, rods, and springs) pushes through the filter and ejects the plug from the plug cutter. The plug falls into the core chute; juice has already left via the collectors.
  5. Outflow / disposal — Peel chute and core chutes receive peel and plugs; augers carry both away separately for further processing.

Drive and structure

  • Drivetrain — Motor (WEG 22) and reducer (Sumitomo Cyclo 6000) drive a belt and gear train. Output goes to cam bearings (shaft extensions) that connect to the transmission.
  • Transmission — Yoke and two 35 mm drive shafts with LMK35UU linear bearings convert rotary motion into the linear stroke that drives extraction (driven bracket, peelers) and the plug ejection cycle.
  • Frame — Main frame carries three interface plates: loaded mount plate (collection + static side of extraction), transmission mount plate (yoke, shafts, bearings), and drivetrain mount plate. Leveling mounts at each leg.
  • Enclosure / shell — Plunger cover and drivetrain cover protect users; overcentered hinges hold lids open or shut. The feeder is also hinged for access and CIP.

Subassembly hierarchy

On the main index, each subsystem (1–11) lists its subassemblies as indented sub-items. For example:

  • Under 1. Intake feeder: Feeding subassembly (with its own page and CAD views).
  • Under 2. Extraction & synchronisation: Fruit support, driven peelers, driven bracket, chute deflectors.
  • Under 4. Collection: Static peelers, juice collectors, plug cutter, static deflectors.
  • Under 5. Plug ejection: Plunger assembly, juice outflow.
  • Under 6. Drivetrain: Motor, reducer, gear train.
  • Under 7. Outflow / disposal: Peel chute, core chutes, augers.
  • Under 8. Frame: Main frame, loaded mount plate, transmission mount plate, drivetrain mount plate.

Detail and interfaces for subassemblies are owner-provided; final tolerancing and assembly are for contractors. Use the Questions for RFP list to flush out parameters per subsystem and subassembly.
